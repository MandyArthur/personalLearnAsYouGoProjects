About us

Introducing Our Amazing Tutoring Services

If you or your child are struggling with lessons or you feel you could benefit from extra support and guidance with Maths, English or Science, then we are the people to call.

Our team has a wealth of experience and we offer a wide range of support packages and tutoring services so whatever your needs we can help! We offer a personalised and friendly approach in all our services and aim to deliver an enlightening tutoring experience.

“The function of education is to teach one to think intensively and to think critically. Intelligence plus character - Martin Luther King, Jr that is the goal of true education” and that is what we believe.

“Children must be taught how to think, not what to think” – Margaret Mead

We work with a small team of subject specialist tutors and believe that the ‘one size fits all’ teaching and learning system doesn’t work. Our tutors can design and structure learning plans where beneficial, will work closely with guardians by reporting on progress and we will signpost students to detailed services, if required.

How We Operate

Quality tutors shouldn’t just give answers to questions. We take the time to help our students understand the topic so that if a similar question comes up in the future they will have the skills and knowledge to confidently answer it.

"Tell me and I forget, teach me and I may remember, involve me and I learn” – Benjamin Franklin

We help nurture a positive, resilient attitude in students by creating a safe space in lessons where they can ask questions freely while also working towards their goals. This gives them a sense of achievement, an appreciation of the progress they have made by means of self-reflection, and the skills required to attempt exam questions without assistance.

We organize instruction so that students are the producers, not just the consumers, of knowledge. For more information on the benefits of this type of learning, please view this link.

“It’s not what is poured into a student, but what’s planted” – Linda Conway. The new GCSEs target a student’s way of thinking rather than the ability to put an answer on paper.
